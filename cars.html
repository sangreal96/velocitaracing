CARS

VR1

Genesis. The first-born go-kart of Velocita Racing. Made in 2015, this 125cc shifter kart was built like a tank, and could definitely take a hit. It was built by the (then) all-first-year team, who started Velocita. Powered by a 5-speed, 125cc engine from the Bajaj Discover, the VR1 did have its flaws.

Reliability just wasn’t one of them.

The VR1 was put to the test, when it faced relentless abuse during the endurance race of the 3rd Season of the National Kart Racing Championship (NKRC). What’s more, the heavens decided to open during the race. While other go-karts suffered from ceased engines and fried electrics, the VR1 toiled on, lap after lap, without any complaints. As a result, Velocita Racing become the best racing team in our college, surpassing all the senior teams. This was an extremely proud moment in Velocita Racing’s history.

Although most of its parts, including the chassis, have been scrapped, there will always be a place in all our hearts for the VR1.

VR2

They say, the mistakes have the power to turn you into something better than you were before. Knowing all the flaws of the VR1 just made the second-generation team of Velocita Racing stronger. The design was given scrupulous attention to detail.

Each subsystem was designed from ground-up, to be better in every way, then the VR1. With a brand-new chassis, the kart was lighter, faster and more ergonomic than its predecessor.

It is also powered by the same 125cc, 5-speed engine from a Bajaj Discover. While all its prime competitors faced reliability issues, the VR2 rose to the top, yet again, making Velocita Racing the best team in college for the second year in a row.

While the forthcoming generations of Velocita Racing aim to reach greater heights, the VR2 will, forever be a symbol of proof, as to how helpful learning from one’s mistakes can be.
